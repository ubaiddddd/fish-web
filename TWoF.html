Understanding the 3 main types of fish

1. Bony Fish - Osteichthyes

Most fish you see are bony fish. They have skeletons made of bone and live in both fresh and salt water.

Key Features:

  • Skeletons made of bone
  • Gills covered by an operclum
  • Have a swim bladder to control buoyancy
  • Thin overlapping scales

Common Examples:

  • Goldfish
  • Salmon
  • Tuna
  • Mahseer - found in Pakistani rivers
  • Indian Pomfret - popular in karachi seafood
Video: Types Of Boney Fish Explained

2. Cartilaginous Fish - Chondrichthyes

These fish have skeletons made of cartilage instead of bone. Many live in the Arabian sea near Pakistan.

Key Features:

  • Skeleton made of cartilage
  • 5-7 gill slits, no gill cover
  • No swim bladder
  • Rough skin with tiny tooth-like scales

Common examples:

  • Great white shark
  • Manta ray
  • Hammerhead Shark
  • Whale Shark - seen off Karachi coast
Video: Sharks and Rays - What's the Difference?

3. Jawless Fish - Agnatha

The oldest living group of fish. They don't have jaws and use a sucker mouth to feed.

Key Features:

  • No jaws or paired fins
  • Round, sucker-like mouth
  • Skeleton made of cartilage
  • Single nostril on top of head

Common Examples:

  • Lamprey
  • Hagfish

Quick Comparison

Type Skeleton Jaws Example
Bony Fish Bone Yes Salmon, Goldfish, Mahseer
Cartilaginous Fish Cartilage Yes Shark, Ray, Whale Shark
Jawless Fish Cartilage No Lamprey, Hagfish

Bony Fish make up 95% of all fish species. They dominate both freshwater rivers in Pakistan and the ocean.

Cartilaginous Fish are apex predators. Whale Sharks are often spotted near Karachi and are completely harmless to humans.

Jawless Fish are living fossils. They've existed for over 360 million years with almost no change.

Fish Found in Pakistan

  • Mahseer - Bony fish found in rivers of northern Pakistan
  • Indian Pomfret - Popular saltwater bony fish in Karachi markets
  • Whale Shark - Gentle cartilaginous fish in the Arabian Sea
  • Rohu & Catla - Common freshwater bony fish in fish farms
